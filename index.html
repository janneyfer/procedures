Introduction

Welcome to the Med-Peds procedures website! Every year our residents rotate through a very popular procedure elective. The skills and lessons from this rotation will be outlined in this website as a source of reference for future procedures.

Before each procedure review the following:

  1. Contraindications. Common contraindications to central and peripheral line placements include coagulopathy, and thrombocytopenia.
  2. Previous relevant imaging. Review past CXR's and lines.
  3. Medical history of pacemakers and devices. If the pacemaker is on the left, place the IJ line in the opposite side
  4. Keep in mind that the lung is higher on the left. A left IJ will need to be placed slightly higher and the catheter will need to be longer.
